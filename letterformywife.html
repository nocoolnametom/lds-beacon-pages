The Author's Story

The author is your typical LDS husband and father. He was born in the convenant and served an honorable full-time mission. After returning home, he married in the Temple and pretty much lived out the typical Mormon story. In terms of his normality, his favorite food is teriyaki chicken.

In 2009 after the public fanfare alongside the release of the first volumes of the Joseph Smith Papers project, the author began researching LDS history with excitement.

However, over time small issues began to appear in his researching. The author listed his concerns and "put them on the shelf," to employ the popular metaphor. Eventually, the weight of these unresolved and expanding issues led to the author needing to explain to his wife the issues he was facing along with the context of the issues in 2016.

That letter formed the foundation of this document, which the author continues to update both with new information as well as resolving mistakes and errors brought to his attention. Accuracy and facts are important, especially with a subject of such deep and potentially eternal impact.

In 2017, the unofficial LDS apologetic group FairMormon publicly released an analysis of Letter For My Wife. As a measurement of the claims, FAIR declared that one-third of the claims they chose to examine were accurate, and that the remaining percentage was not false though still open to debate. AS the author has pointed out, FAIR has not evaluated all of the letter, and the amount of the letter that FAIR has chosen to either uphold as accurate or not commented on is easily the majority of the letter.
